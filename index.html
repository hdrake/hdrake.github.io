    Research Interests

    I am currently a research assistant in physical oceanography in the department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences (AOS) at Princeton University. My research aims at understanding the overturning circulation of the oceans and their role in the greater climate system. In particular, I am currently interested in the role of the Southern Ocean in the adiabtic upwelling of North Atlantic and Indo-Pacific deep waters. I am also interested in how these upwelling pathways regulate the climate, particularly through heat uptake, carbon uptake, sea-ice feedbacks, and nutrient upwelling.

    Incoming Graduate Student

    In fall 2016, I will be starting a PhD in Physical Oceanography advised by Raffaele Ferrari at MIT/WHOI.
